### The Three Logical Bonds

| Bond Type | Cognitive Function | Physical Analogy |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Deep Reasoning | Strong Morphisms: Strongly compositional steps where failure invalidates the entire trajectory. | Covalent Bonds: Primary high-energy molecular structure. |
| Self-Reflection | Endomorphic Morphisms: Long-range consistency constraints across the diagram. | Hydrogen Bonds: Long-range folding and stabilization. |

These bonds must fold through a funnel to reach functional intelligence.

## 4. Semantic Isomers: Why the Inner Shape of Thought Matters

Two reasoning trajectories can reach the same colimit (answer) while possessing radically different internal bond topologies. These are **Semantic Isomers**.

**The Isomer Rule**: Because logic is history-sensitive, mixing incompatible isomers forms convex combinations of non-compatible colimits, driving the system out of the manifold of foldable configurations.

### The Distillation Puzzle

Distilling a giant model into a smaller one often fails because it copies the trace without the history—like reconstructing a protein fold from a flat ingredient list.

**Failure Modes of Isomer Mixing**

- Structural Frustration: Internal spins misalign; logic shatters under perturbation.  
- Marginalization Loss: Summarization integrates out stabilizing morphisms.  
- Non-Distillability: Intelligence belongs to the diagram (history), not just the colimit (result).
