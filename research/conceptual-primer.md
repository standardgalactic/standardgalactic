# RSVP and TARTAN: A Learner’s Guide to the Architecture of Reality

## 1. Introduction: The Lens of Coarse-Graining

Imagine observing a storm from a satellite. You see a grand, swirling vortex—a stable, predictable shape. You do not see the billion individual collisions of water molecules or the local fluctuations in air pressure that constitute the storm's microstate. This is the essence of coarse-graining: compressing high-dimensional data into a lower-dimensional, usable interface.

In this framework, reality is not composed of discrete objects. What we call physics, thought, and economics are coarse-grained projections of a deeper deterministic substrate. This guide provides the conceptual scaffolding needed to understand how a complex field is filtered into the world we navigate.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this primer, you will be able to:

1. characterize the RSVP substrate as a constrained triple of coupled fields
