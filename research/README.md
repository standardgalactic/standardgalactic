# The Architecture of Coarse Grained Projection Systems

[From Field Dynamics to Extractive Systems](https://standardgalactic.github.io/standardgalactic/field_dynamics.pdf)

* [The Physics of Toxic Digital Platforms](https://standardgalactic.github.io/standardgalactic/research/)

This project develops a unified theoretical framework proposing that physics, cognition, and economics are governed by the same underlying structural laws.

At its core is the concept of **coarse graining**: the compression of high-dimensional systems into simplified, observable interfaces. Rather than treating physical systems, minds, and markets as fundamentally distinct, this framework argues they are different projections of the same underlying dynamics.

Using the RSVP (Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum) model, the work formalizes systems as structured trajectories subject to consistency conditions. Observable phenomena—including quantum-like behavior, cognition, and economic coordination—are treated as the result of how these trajectories are projected, compressed, and stabilized.

## Core Idea

All systems can be understood as projections of high-dimensional trajectories into constrained, observable spaces.

Coarse graining determines:

- what information is preserved  
- what is discarded  
- what becomes actionable  

Different domains correspond to different projection regimes, not different underlying realities.

---

## Extractive vs Non Extractive Systems

Modern digital systems are described as **pathological projections**.

They:

- fragment trajectories  
- prevent completion  
- extract value from partial states  

This creates systems that profit from instability rather than resolution.

A non-extractive system must instead:

- preserve trajectory coherence  
- align incentives with completion  
- maintain consistency across projections  

---

## Objective

The goal of this project is to define architectures where:

- projections remain coherent  
- trajectories can complete  
- value is generated through stabilization rather than fragmentation  

This requires rethinking economic systems, computational models, and cognitive representations as instances of the same structural process.

---

## Final Statement

The distinctions between physics, cognition, and economics are not fundamental.

They are artifacts of how we project and compress reality.

Understanding and redesigning these projection systems is the key to building stable, non-extractive infrastructures.
